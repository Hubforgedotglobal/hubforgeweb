Glocal Impact Awards 2026

Reimagining recognition in the world of evidence

Evidence belongs to everyone

The Glocal Impact Awards 2026 celebrate a simple truth: anyone can be a champion of evidence for good. You don't need a fancy degree, a prestigious title, or a traditional M&E role to create meaningful change through data and learning.

We're recognizing individuals, institutions, and communities around the world who are building, innovating, or using evidence to create better programs, amplify voices, and shape smarter policies—regardless of their background or affiliation. This is about honoring the spirit of "global + local" action and proving that evidence work happens everywhere.

Who We Celebrate

The Glocal Impact Awards 2026 honor changemakers from all backgrounds—individuals, institutions, and communities innovating with evidence for good.

Community Changemakers

People using data and learning to improve programs in their communities—whether they're youth organizers, health workers, educators, or grassroots leaders.

Field Innovators

Practitioners experimenting with new approaches to data collection, evidence generation, or community-driven learning—proving creativity matters more than credentials.

Institutional Pioneers

Organizations, networks, or institutions embedding evidence into their DNA—not because it's required, but because they believe in learning and accountability.

Collaborative Champions

Partnerships, collectives, or movements building evidence ecosystems together—showing that shared learning creates stronger impact than working alone.

Why This Matters

Redefine What Counts as Expertise

Degrees and titles don't define who can do great evidence work. We're celebrating impact, innovation, and dedication—wherever they come from.

Amplify Underrepresented Voices

Too many brilliant changemakers remain invisible in global conversations. These awards shine a light on people and communities doing transformative work.

Inspire Through Real Stories

When you see someone like you using evidence to create change, it becomes possible. These stories prove that evidence work happens everywhere.

Build a More Inclusive Movement

Evidence shouldn't be gatekept by the privileged few. By celebrating diverse champions, we're building a movement where everyone belongs.
