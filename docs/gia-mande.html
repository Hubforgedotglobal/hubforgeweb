About the Glocal Impact Awards

The Glocal Impact Awards for Monitoring & Evaluation (GIA-M&E) celebrate changemakers who champion evidence-based practices at the grassroots level. Too often, local leaders and organizations doing exceptional work remain unrecognized in global evaluation spaces.

GIA-M&E highlights and honors these evidence champions, amplifying their voices, showcasing their innovations, and encouraging others to build strong, evidence-based practices in their communities.

The awards are part of our vision to elevate global leadership in evaluation and ensure that those closest to the challenges are also recognized for driving solutions.

Award Focus

  • Level: Grassroots & Community
  • Approach: Glocal (Global + Local)
  • Recognition: Evidence champions
  • Impact: Amplify local voices

Why the Glocal Impact Awards Matter

Elevate Local Heroes

Shine a spotlight on community leaders, grassroots organizations, and local champions who are driving evidence-based change but often go unnoticed on global stages.

Inspire Others

Showcase success stories and innovative practices that inspire other organizations and communities to adopt evidence-based approaches in their own work.

Bridge Global & Local

Connect grassroots innovators with global evaluation networks, creating opportunities for learning, collaboration, and mutual growth across borders.

Strengthen the Field

Build recognition and credibility for MERL practices at the grassroots, encouraging more organizations to invest in evidence generation and use.

Validate Local Wisdom

Acknowledge that communities closest to the challenges often have the deepest insights and most innovative solutions worth celebrating and learning from.

Create Role Models

Develop a growing network of recognized practitioners who serve as mentors, advisors, and role models for emerging evidence champions in their regions.

Award Categories

Individual Champion

Recognizing individuals who have demonstrated exceptional commitment to building and using evidence for social impact at the community level.

  • Leadership in MERL practices
  • Innovation in data collection or use
  • Capacity building contributions
  • Community-level impact
Grassroots Organization

Honoring community-based organizations that have embedded strong MERL practices into their programs and achieved measurable impact.

  • Evidence-based programming
  • Adaptive learning culture
  • Community engagement
  • Sustainable MERL systems

Youth Innovator

Celebrating young changemakers under 35 who are championing evidence-based approaches in their communities and inspiring peers.

  • Youth-led initiatives
  • Creative MERL approaches
  • Peer influence & mentorship
  • Future-focused practices

Collaborative Impact

Recognizing partnerships or networks that have strengthened evidence ecosystems through collaboration, resource sharing, and collective learning.

  • Multi-stakeholder collaboration
  • Knowledge sharing platforms
  • Collective impact measurement
  • Network strengthening
